GLACIALIS CREAMERY

Logo, branding, and packaging for a fictitious ice cream shoppe. The name is taken from the Latin term for the word, "frozen."

Glacialis Creamery is a small family-owned and operated premium ice cream shoppe that crafts their ice cream in small batches on a daily basis. Customers are usually expected to sit in to enjoy dessert, however, there are packed single serving cups (4 oz.) to purchase and take home as well. The packaging derives a hexagonal shape from the pattern of honeycomb, representing the close knit process of how the company crafts their ice cream. Containers will have different colored labels that correspond to the flavor inside.

In addition to the pre-packed cups to take home, Glacialis Creamery also offers an option in which the customer can choose what flavor(s) they want to pack. This option will be packed in half of the single serving size (2 oz.) so that the customer can either take home a greater variety of flavors, or share one with a friend.

The labels are all either white or pink in color scheme with a space on top of the lid to write in the name of the flavor. A paper ribbon to holds it together and provide a handle for ease of carrying. Upon releasing the paper ribbon, the two halves open to reveal quotes regarding ice cream or sharing
