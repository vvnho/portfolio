COLLABORATION WITH BIOMEDICAL ENGINEERS

In Spring 2013, a fellow designer and I teamed up with a group of biomedical engineers to promote their senior design project. This group was creating a new way to cryopreserve oocytes, primarily for chimpanzees.

In Spring 2014, a different fellow designer and I teamed up with two other groups of biomedical engineers to help with their graphical promotional materials. One group designed a way to analyze blood sodium levels in a portable way and the other group designed a better way to grow and transport stem cells for human hearts.

OOCYTE CRYOPRESERVATION

In collaboration with: Winnie Siu

IDENTITY

The name "Vitricyte" is derived from combining "in vitro" with "oocyte". A circular copper mesh screen is used in their device and that form was abstracted to in the logomark.

ANIMATION

The engineers asked us to help them create a breif animation that showed how their device worked.

The devices were drawn in Adobe Illustrator CS6 and the animation was created in Adobe After Effects CS6.

STEM CELL BIOREACTOR

In collaboration with: Mandy Chew

IDENTITY

The engineers use a hexagon grid in their device for growing stem cells for the human heart and thus, these forms are present in the logomark.
